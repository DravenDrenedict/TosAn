Darwin’s theories has coined the term, “The Survival Of The Fittest”.
This book is an academic explanation of Darwin’s many theories which ultimately becoming the concept of Life Sciences now.

Darwin’s theory of evolution is based on key facts and the inferences drawn from them summarised as follows:

  • Every species is fertile enough to grow the population.
  • Populations remain roughly the same size.
  • Resources are limited and stable over time.
  • A struggle for survival ensues.
  • Individuals vary from one another.
  • Much variation is inheritable.
  • Less-suited individuals are less likely to survive and reproduce.
  • Suited individuals leave traits to future generations.
  • Populations change and adapt, forming new species.
